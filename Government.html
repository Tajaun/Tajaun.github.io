How Does Disruptive Technolgy help the Government ?

The notion that the public sector can't or won't innovate is a myth. Innovation in government occurs virtually every day from the way governments across the world are opening up their data to entrepreneurs to build apps for everything from real time transit information to school test score comparisons to the myriad ways soldiers on the battlefield innovate address life and death challenges. Disruptive technologies have largely modified the way that we now work. Not so long ago, it took an entire room to fit a computer. Then, with the emergence of the microprocessor, there came the birth of desktops and personal computers. With these new enhancements, the World Wide Web came into being allowing access to insurmountable information which greatly benefited the Government. The Government with the aid of disruptive technology has bee able to monitor economy and the country. Elections and other campaigns are better managed with the use of such technologies. Government innovation is rarely if ever disruptive. Instead, it typically represents what is called sustaining innovation. Sustaining innovation can improve existing products or services, typically adding performance but at a higher cost and typically greater complexity. Some sustaining innovations are incremental, year-to-year improvements. Others are dramatic, such as the new breakthrough business models that emerged from the transition from analog to digital telecommunications, and from digital to optical.

Benefits of Having Disruptive Technolgy

  • It helps with productivity.
  • It produces higher productivity gains.
  • It makes the workforce happier.
  • Work can be done outside the office.
  • Enhance capabilities: Public safety officers respond faster and more proactively to emergencies
  • Engage partners: Localities build an ecosystem for water conservation and security

Disruptive Technology can increase value by both collecting better information about how effectively public servants, programs, and policies are addressing mission challenges, as well as helping government deliver services based on real-time and situation-specific conditions.

Examples of Disruptive Technology

  • Electronic Voting- Instead of casting your vote on a paper this can be done electronically.
  • Computer Assisted Survey Information Collection Machine- This Reduces the need for the manual method of getting information from citizens by the Government agents.
  • SmartPhones- Meetings and important information can be transmitted using this device.

The Evolution of Disruptive Technology in the Government Sector
Action Past Methods Disruptive Method
Create Data Is Collected through analog means Sensor Captures information without user input.
Communicate Data is manually developed. Data is seamlessly transmitted among objects to a central point.
Aggregate Reports are developed and a manager makes an analysis of what is important. Data is sorted as a result of new technology.(No need for human analysis).
Analyze An analyst comes to a conslusion about the information that was collected. Amplified intelligence detects patterns across disparate data points and previously unrelated events.
Act Data is presented to a decision maker who instructs future procedure. Real time signals make insights actionable or it directly initiate an action.

The Cycle of The Disruptive Evolution

Displaced Market for each of the technologies named above are:

  • Electronic voting is replacing the method of casting votes on ballots(hard copy paper).
  • Computer Assisted Survey Information Collection Machine replaces the use of agents to collect information.
  • SmartPhones replaces the need to send out memos in textual format to workers in the office.
